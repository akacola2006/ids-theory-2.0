IDS Theory

Information-Driven Stress, Debug, and Self-organization

IDS theory is a mathematical framework for open hierarchical systems that observe the world with finite resources, compress what they see, and accumulate tension where their current description fails. Its central object is not a number but an equivalence class: the non-integrable residual that coarse-graining cannot erase.

The mathematical heart of the theory is the residual class [ω] ∈ C¹ ∕ im d₀ — the part of an observed flow that no local potential can produce. Stress is the pressure of what the current description cannot resolve; debugging is the reorganization, not the deletion, of that residue; resilience is its capacity to persist across perturbation, observation, and coarse-graining.

The editions below present the theory at different depths. The full canonical text (Japanese) is the authoritative source; the public edition is a condensed, general-audience account that keeps the axioms, the principal theorems, the falsifiability commitments, and the theory's honest boundaries.
