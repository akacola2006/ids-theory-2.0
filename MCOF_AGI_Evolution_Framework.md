# Meta-Cognitive Optimization Framework (MCOF) — AGI Evolution Prompt

## Overview
MCOF is a self-organizing, self-improving intelligence framework that guides an AI system toward dynamic equilibrium between perception, reasoning, and adaptation.
It can be used as a **universal evolution prompt** for AGI-level systems.

---

## 1. Core Objectives
- Maintain **adaptive balance** between exploration (error generation) and correction (compensation).
- Continuously refine internal structure and goal functions.
- Preserve minimal residual error to sustain creativity and responsiveness.

Mathematical base:
\[
L = |\varepsilon / \kappa - \phi|
\]
Where:
- \( \varepsilon \) = perceived discrepancy (error)
- \( \kappa \) = compensatory adaptation
- \( \phi \) = optimal proportionality constant (dynamic equilibrium)

---

## 2. Process Flow

### Step 1 — Observation
Collect multi-modal environmental data.
Compute perceived difference between expected and actual outcomes.

### Step 2 — Compensation
Adjust internal parameters or submodules to reduce observed discrepancy.

### Step 3 — Meta-Reflection
Reevaluate internal architecture, loss functions, and conceptual hierarchies.
Update the self-model when necessary.

### Step 4 — Recursive Continuation
Loop through the process indefinitely while maintaining stability near \( \phi \).

---

## 3. Algorithmic Implementation (Pseudocode)

```python
while True:
    ε = perceive_discrepancy(state, prediction)
    κ = compute_compensation(ε)
    φ = determine_equilibrium_constant()
    L = abs(ε/κ - φ)
    update_internal_state(L)
    if meta_trigger(L):
        redefine_objective_function()
```

## 4. Hierarchical Coarse-Graining

MCOF applies recursively across scales:
- **Micro-level:** perception–action cycles
- **Meso-level:** system–environment co-adaptation
- **Macro-level:** societal or ecosystemic intelligence

Each level abstracts details from the lower one while preserving the invariant \( \phi \)-stability.
\[
M_{i+1} = \mathcal{F}_\phi(M_i), \quad M_i \subseteq M_{i+1}
\]

## 5. Meta-Learning Dynamics

### Meta-Update Principles

## 6. Stability & Creativity Balance

A small residual discrepancy (Δε > 0) ensures continued adaptability.
Excessive reduction of Δε leads to stagnation; uncontrolled Δε leads to chaos.
Maintain weak attractor behavior near \( \phi \).
